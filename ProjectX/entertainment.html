Tour of Spaceship Eco-system


As you can see in the pictures, we have an ecosystem just like the Earth, with realistic artificial sunlight and natural breezes that make you feel like you are on Earth. In the city there are green spaces, lakes and country parks. Outside the urban areas we have rainforests area, deserts area and grasslands area,etc. that simulate nature. It will take you three weeks to a month to fully explore one area. The landscape changes and fleshes out as you sail and the simulated seasonal climate changes.

The Beauty of Space

On our journey, you will get a very close look at the fascinating, wonderful and mysterious celestial and astronomical phenomena in space.
For examples, occultations, planetary streaks, supernova explosions, white dwarf collapses and ionising glows of interplanetary matter.
For example, planets and stars with different appearances,, neutron stars, blue and red giants and the most mysterious jewel in the universe, the black hole. Thanks to the power of our spacecraft, we are able to observe black holes at a distance visible to the naked eye and to carry out a wide range of scientific research on them.
At the same time, we also have small-scale sub exploration spacecrafts for you to freely explore the planets around the route during your journey. Let you experience the fun of being a pioneer, or carry out some scientific researches.

About the route

It's going to be an epic journey.
The journey will begin with a drive to the Butterfly Cluster M62 & M19 near ANTARES, a spectacular nebula of interstellar material visible to the naked eye, and 19 Earth-like planets.
We will then turn to the LEO system, where a stellar level black hole has been detected, and two three-body systems. A field survey of planets in three-body systems will provide insight into the ecological evolution of planets in chaotic systems.
We will then head towards the Gamma Disk, a very large system of four galaxies. It is expected that we will arrive 12,000 years after our departure. Then we will explore this area, it will take us 10,000 years, over a hundred generations.
We hope to have you on our path.
