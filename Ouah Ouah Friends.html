Ouah Ouah Friends

Ouah Ouah Friends is a UX research project that applies a set of HCI(human-computer interaction) theories, methods and techniques to investigate the pet community. It aims to improve community cohesion and activity, and help pet owners be connected to each other to have long-term support for their pets. By analysing the research data, we located the positive and negative sides of being in the pet community. We developed an initial design concept to improve the user experience of the pet community.
My role: UX Researcher
Tool used: Miro, Slack, Zotero
What I did: User Experience research
Duration: 28 July - 11 November 2022 as part of Course Advanced Human-Computer Interaction at UQ Check the project report

The background

The course requires each team to conduct an inquiry into studying a specific real-world setting, where they will apply a set of HCI theories, methods and techniques to understand people and their specific settings. The course provides three project domains based on real-world settings
In speed-dating, four of us interested in the community domain came together because of our love for pets. The target community of our team's research fell on the pet community. We had already had some contact with the pet community and pet owners, plus our initial background research.
Our initial research question for the pet community was to enhance the cohesion and activity of the pet community. After further research, the question was specified: How can pet owners be connected to each other to have long-term support for their pets?

Literature Review

Through a review of more than twenty relevant papers, we reveal fundamental insights into our research question from three topics:
  • The Sense of Community
  • The Need for a Pet Community
  • Improving the Attraction to a Community
Literature reviews helped us to justify the need for more involved research, helped to specify the research questions, indicated possible research directions and improved the overall credibility of future research findings.

Methodology

  • Contextual Interview
    • Photo elicitation
    • Mapping
  • Observation
  • Digital Ethnography
According to the say-make-do model, we involved five different research method. 6 participants responded to our contextual interviews, and we observed more than 20 pet owners during the observation and digital ethnography. We were using Miro to conduct the interview and process the data.
We gained insight into the existing pet owner community's pain points and a deeper understanding of what pet owners need from the community by connecting with our target users and analysing the results.

Result & Conclusion

In this project, we aim to help pet owners be connected to have long term support for their pets. We explore the pet owners' reasons for joining the communities and participating in the relevant activities through conducting diverse HCI methods.
First, we conduct contextual interviews to explore the users' pain points. Photo elicitation is one of the contextual interview methods. Since we find out the pet owner is willing to share their pet story, we adopt this method to motivate them to provide insight into the pet community.
We also use the mapping method. The mapping method makes the participants draw their connection as the map is down. We believe this method is suitable for researching the relationship between the owners and the community. We also can understand the pet owners' attitudes toward each community.
Additionally, digital ethnography has also been adopted. Through this method, we can obtain data about the insights that the pet owner joins the communities.
After evaluating our data, we conclude the reasons and the pain points of the pet owners joining the community:
  • Motivation for joining the pet community
    • Entertainment
    • Seeking Support
    • Seeking Recommendations
    • Social Network
  • Pain Points
    • Uncontrolled Environment
    • Information Quality
